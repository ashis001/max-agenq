# Buy a Policy — Workflow (Storyboarding / Product Flow)

This document outlines the interactive, guided experience for a user purchasing a new insurance policy through NINA.

---

### Scene 1 — Entry (Natural Start)
**User clicks “Ask NINA”**

**User:**
“I’m looking to buy health insurance.”

---

### Scene 2 — Context Awareness (Subtle, Not Flashy)
**NINA:**
“Sure, I can help with that.

I found your profile — John, 32, based in Toronto. Just confirming, is this still correct?”

**User:**
“Yes.”

👉 *(Feels real. No hype. No friction.)*

---

### Scene 3 — Light Personalization (Not Interrogation)
**NINA:**
“Great.

I just need a bit more context to narrow things down.

Are you married? And how would you describe your lifestyle — more active, or more social/travel-heavy?”

**User:**
“Married. Fairly social.”

---

### Scene 4 — Set Expectation (Very Important)
**NINA:**
“Got it.

I’ll shortlist a few plans that fit your profile, and then we can go deeper based on what matters most to you.”

👉 *(This sets the flow clearly — feels like a guided experience)*

---

### Scene 5 — Plan Comparison (Core Product Moment)
*(UI shows a comparison table — 3–4 plans)*

**NINA:**
“I’ve narrowed this down to 4 plans that fit well for you.

Here’s a quick view comparing coverage, premium, and key benefits.

Would you like me to walk you through the differences, or recommend one based on your profile?”

| Plan | Monthly Premium | Coverage Limit | Key Benefits | Limitations | Best For |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Bronze | $29 | $50K | Basic hospitalization | No outpatient, no critical care | Budget / minimal coverage |
| Silver | $59 | $100K | Specialist visits, health wallet | Limited critical illness | Balanced, most common choice |
| Gold | $69 | $250K | Critical illness, higher limits | Slightly higher premium | Families, long-term protection |
| Platinum | $129 | Unlimited | Zero co-pay, global coverage | Highest cost | Premium, no-compromise coverage |

---

### Scene 6 — Recommendation (Human, Not Pushy)
**User:**
“I think Silver looks good. What do you think?”

**NINA:**
“Silver is a good option — it’s quite popular.

But given your profile — married and fairly active lifestyle — I’d lean towards **Gold**.

For a small increase in monthly cost, you get:
- Better coverage limits
- Critical illness protection
- More flexibility long-term

It really depends on how much coverage you’re comfortable with, but Gold gives you more peace of mind.”

---

### Scene 7 — Exploration (Feels Real, Not Linear)
**User:**
“What about Platinum?”

**NINA:**
“Platinum is more of a premium option.

It’s usually chosen by users who want maximum coverage and don’t want to worry about edge cases.

Think of it this way:
- **Gold** → strong, balanced
- **Platinum** → full coverage, fewer trade-offs

If budget isn’t a concern, Platinum is great. Otherwise, Gold is already a very solid choice.”

---

### Scene 8 — Action (Simple, Not Overdramatic)
**NINA:**
“Would you like me to generate a quote for **Gold**?”

**User:**
“Yes.”

*(Small UI feedback / loader)*

**NINA:**
“Done.

I’ve created your quote and sent it to your email.

You’ll also see:
- A simple summary of coverage
- Key inclusions and exclusions
- A direct link to continue the application

You don’t need to go through long documents unless you want to.”

---

### Scene 9 — Close (Natural Ending)
**NINA:**
“Let me know if you’d like help completing the application or comparing more options.”

**User:**
“Not right now.”

**NINA:**
“No problem. I’m here whenever you need.”
